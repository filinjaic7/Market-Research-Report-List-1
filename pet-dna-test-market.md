<p><p>The pet DNA test market is primarily categorized into saliva tests and stool tests. Saliva tests involve collecting a sample from the pet's mouth, which is then analyzed for genetic information, offering insights into breed composition, health traits, and inheritance patterns. In contrast, stool tests analyze fecal samples to detect specific genetic markers associated with diseases or ancestry. Both testing types cater to pet owners' needs for understanding their pets’ genetics, health risks, and lineage, contributing to informed care decisions.</p></p>
