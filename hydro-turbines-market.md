<p><p>Hydro turbines are categorized into several types based on their design and operational principles, each suited for different water flow conditions. Francis turbines are versatile and used in medium to high-head applications, while Kaplan turbines excel in low-head scenarios with variable flow. Pelton turbines are designed for high-head, low-flow conditions, using impulse to convert energy. Additionally, other specialized turbine types like cross-flow and bulb turbines cater to unique site requirements, expanding the market for hydroelectric power generation.</p></p>
