<p><p>The patient body temperature monitoring market is divided into non-invasive and invasive segments. Non-invasive temperature monitoring employs surface techniques like infrared thermometers and electronic skin patches, providing quick and painless readings. Invasive temperature monitoring involves more direct methods, such as rectal, bladder, or esophageal thermometers, offering higher accuracy and real-time monitoring, particularly in critical care settings. Both types cater to different healthcare needs, balancing comfort and precision for optimal patient care.</p></p>
