<p><p>The aluminum extrusion manufacturing market encompasses two primary types: cold extrusion and hot extrusion. Cold extrusion involves shaping aluminum at room temperature, resulting in higher strength and finer surface finishes, making it ideal for precision components. In contrast, hot extrusion is performed at elevated temperatures, allowing for easier shaping of thicker materials and complex profiles, but may lead to surface oxidation. Both methods cater to diverse industries, including automotive, construction, and aerospace, each addressing specific manufacturing needs and applications.</p></p>
