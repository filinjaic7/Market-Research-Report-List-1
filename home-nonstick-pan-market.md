<p><p>The home nonstick pan market primarily features two types: PTFE and ceramic. PTFE pans, known for their durable nonstick coating, provide excellent heat distribution, making them ideal for various cooking techniques. However, they require careful handling to avoid scratching. In contrast, ceramic pans offer a more natural nonstick surface, free from chemicals, and are preferred for their eco-friendliness. While they can withstand higher temperatures, they may wear out faster than PTFE options, influencing consumer choices based on health and performance.</p></p>
